Solar Q & A :
What is a planet?

A planet orbits the Sun, is massive enough for its gravity to make it nearly round, and has cleared its orbital neighborhood of other debris.

DefinitionTerms
What is a dwarf planet?

A dwarf planet orbits the Sun and is nearly round but has not cleared its orbital path of other objects. It is not a moon.

Examples include Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, and Ceres.

Dwarf PlanetsDefinition
What is a moon (natural satellite)?

A moon is a natural object that orbits a planet or dwarf planet. Moons vary widely in size, composition, and activity.

MoonsDefinition
What is the asteroid belt?

The asteroid belt is a region between Mars and Jupiter containing many rocky bodies, including the dwarf planet Ceres.

Belts & Regions
What is the Kuiper Belt?

The Kuiper Belt is a distant, disk-like region beyond Neptune filled with icy objects. Pluto, Haumea, Makemake, and many comets originate here.

Kuiper BeltOuter Solar System
What is the Oort Cloud?

The Oort Cloud is a hypothetical spherical shell of icy bodies far beyond the Kuiper Belt, thought to be the source of long-period comets.

Oort CloudOuter Solar System
Why does Mercury have extreme temperatures?

Mercury’s thin exosphere and slow rotation allow heat to escape at night and build during the day, creating huge temperature swings.

MercuryAtmosphere
Why is Venus so hot?

Venus has a runaway greenhouse effect: a thick CO₂ atmosphere and sulfuric-acid clouds trap heat, making it hotter than Mercury.

VenusGreenhouse Effect
What causes ocean tides on Earth?

Tides are primarily caused by the Moon’s gravity, with the Sun providing a secondary effect. Alignment during full/new moons creates stronger spring tides.

EarthTides
Why is Mars called the Red Planet?

Its soil contains iron oxides (rust) that reflect reddish light, giving Mars its distinctive color.

MarsSurface
What is Jupiter’s Great Red Spot?

A gigantic, long-lived storm larger than Earth, driven by Jupiter’s turbulent atmosphere and strong jet streams.

JupiterStorms
What are Saturn’s rings made of?

Mostly water-ice particles mixed with dust and rock, ranging from tiny grains to house-sized chunks.

SaturnRings
Why does Uranus rotate on its side?

Uranus is tilted by ~98°, likely due to a massive ancient collision, causing extreme seasons that last decades.

UranusTilt
Why is Neptune deep blue?

Methane in its atmosphere absorbs red light and reflects blue. Powerful winds shape dark storm systems.

NeptuneAtmosphere
Does the Moon have an atmosphere?

The Moon has an extremely thin exosphere, not a breathable atmosphere, so temperatures vary widely and sound cannot travel.

MoonExosphere
Why is Io so volcanically active?

Strong tidal forces from Jupiter and neighboring moons flex Io’s interior, generating heat that fuels hundreds of volcanoes.

IoTidal Heating
Could life exist in Europa’s ocean?

Europa likely has a salty subsurface ocean warmed by tidal heating. If chemical energy is available, microbial life is considered possible.

EuropaSubsurface Ocean
What makes Titan unique?

Titan has a thick nitrogen atmosphere and surface lakes and seas of liquid methane/ethane—an Earth-like hydrological cycle with hydrocarbons.

TitanAtmosphere
Does any moon have a magnetic field?

Ganymede—the largest moon—has its own intrinsic magnetic field, unique among moons.

GanymedeMagnetism
Why does Triton orbit backward?

Triton orbits Neptune in a retrograde (opposite) direction, suggesting it was captured from the Kuiper Belt rather than forming in place.

TritonRetrograde Orbit
Will Phobos crash into Mars?

Phobos is slowly spiraling inward and may break apart into a ring or impact Mars in tens of millions of years.

PhobosOrbital Decay
What is a light‑year?

A light‑year is a unit of distance: how far light travels in one year (~9.46 trillion km), not a unit of time.

UnitsDistance
How massive is the Sun compared to the Solar System?

The Sun contains about 99.8% of the Solar System’s mass; everything else (planets, moons, comets) share the remainder.

SunMass
What is a comet?

A comet is an icy body that releases gas and dust when heated by the Sun, forming a glowing coma and one or more tails.

CometsIcy Bodies
Why isn’t Pluto a planet anymore?

Pluto didn’t meet the “cleared its orbit” criterion set in 2006, so it was reclassified as a dwarf planet.

PlutoClassification
Do other planets have rings?

Yes. All four giant planets—Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune—have ring systems, though Saturn’s are the most prominent.

RingsGiants
Who discovered Uranus?

William Herschel discovered Uranus in 1781—the first planet found with a telescope.

HistoryDiscoveries
Is a day on Venus longer than its year?

Yes. Venus rotates so slowly that one Venus day (~243 Earth days) is longer than its year (~225 Earth days).

VenusRotation
